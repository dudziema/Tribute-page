Here is timeline of Elon's life:

  • 1971 - Born in Pretoria, South Africa.

  • 1984 - Sold the code of a BASIC-based video game he created called Blastar to PC and Office Technology magazine for approximately $500.

  • 1995 - Received his Bechelor in Physics and Economics from the University of Pennsylvania.

  • 1995 - Moved to California and applied for physics at Stanford University. However, he left the program after two days.

  • 1999 - Started Zip2, a web software company with father's money (28 000 USD).

  • 1999 - Compaq acquired Zip2 for $307 million in cash and Musk received $22 million for his 7-percent share.

  • 1999 - Musk co-founded X.com, an online financial services and e-mail payment company.

  • 2001 - Musk became involved with the nonprofit Mars Society. He was inspired by plans to place a growth-chamber for plants on Mars and discussed funding the project himself.

  • 2002 - With $100 million of his early fortune, Musk founded Space Exploration Technologies Corp., traded as SpaceX. He remains the company's CEO and also holds the title of Chief Engineer.

  • 2002 - PayPal was acquired by eBay for $1.5 billion in stock, of which Musk—the largest shareholder with 11.7% of shares—received over $100 million.

  • 2004 - Led the Series A round of investment in Tesla Motors, joining Tesla's Board of Directors as Chairman.

  • 2006 - SpaceX attempted its first launch of the Falcon 1 rocket and although the rocket failed to reach Earth orbit, it was awarded a Commercial Orbital Transportation Services program contract from NASA later that year.

  • 2008 - SpaceX succeeded in launching the Falcon 1 into orbit, making it the first private liquid-fuel rocket to do so.

  • 2017 - Musk purchased the domain X.com from PayPal for an undisclosed amount, explaining it has sentimental value.

  • 2018 - SpaceX launched the Falcon Heavy; the inaugural mission carried Musk's personal Tesla Roadster as a dummy payload.

  • 2020 - SpaceX launched its first crewed flight, the Demo-2, becoming the first private company to place a person into orbit and dock a crewed spacecraft with the ISS.
